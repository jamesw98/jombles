Footwork

Without Hand Signals:

One fencer leads the rest of the fencers

The group is supposed to keep distance with the leader while the leader advances and retreats

If the leader lunges, the group waits for him to recover, then lunges and recovers

With Hand Signals:

Same basic idea as without hand

For this drill the leader can raise their left, right, or both hands

Each hand/combo

Each fencer has a glove

Fencers come en guarde at advance lunge distance

Alternating turns, the fencers use an advance lunge to attempt to hit the other

During the attacking fencer's turn, the other may take as many retreats as they want

After getting hit, switch attackers

One fencer has a glove

This drill can done stationary or moving

The fencer without a glove is trying to lunge and grab the glove

The fencer with the glove can move the glove out of the way when the other lunges

When doing this moving, the fencer w/o the glove is trying to manipulate distance

Bladework

This is essentially a bladework version of the smooth drill

The only allowed footwork is advance lunges and retreats

The attacker is allowed on advance lunge in attempt to hit the defending fencer in the shoulder or lower

While the attacker is advance lunging, the defender is allowed as many retreats as they want

Once the attacker recovers, the rolls switch

The only valid footwork in this drill is a lunge

This is essentially a bouting drill

The fencers come en guarde at lunge distance

The fencers are trying to hit each other, using any bladework action they want

The only thing the fencers cannot do, is move their back foot, they can lunge and recover though

(!) NOTE (!) this is mainly an epee drill and requires modification for the other weapons

(!) NOTE (!) this drill assumes right handed fencer vs. right handed fencer

This drill can be done moving or stationary

There are two roles: coach and student

The coach can choose between three cues to give the student

1. extend to the inside of the hand/elbow, cue for circle 6

2. extend to the outside of the hand/elbow, cue for parry 6

3. extend to the chest, cue for parry 4

The student must recognize what the proper parry is and execute a parry and riposte to the proper target

This drill can be done with any parry

There are two roles, attacker and defender

The attacker extends to a line and advances

The defender parry and retreats

This repeats two more times, except on the third time, the defender does not retreat and ripostes

There are two roles, attacker and defender

The attacker can only attack, no retreating

The defender can only defend, no attacks except counters or attack in prep

This is essentially a bouting drill, once a fencer is hit, reset distance and start again

Switch roles once 5 total touches have been scored

This is a bouting drill

One fencer starts with two touches, the other starts with none

Set a timer for 60, 30, or 20 seconds

The goal of the drill is for the fencer with no touches to tie the score

If the fencer that started with no touches gets two touches without the other fencer scoring, they win

If the fencer that started with touches ends up with more touches than the other with no time left, they win

If the fencer that started with touches gets a touche, the fencer that started with none now has to match that score

There are three levels of progression for this drill

Each level has an attacker an a defender

At each level, no one is really leading footwork, but the attacker is trying to get close enough to attack

Level 1:

The only action the attacker has is a lunge to any target

The only action the defender has is a retreat to get away from the lunge

The goal of the attacker is to manipulate distance so they can hit the defender

The goal of the defender is to not get hit

Level 2:

Building off of level 1

The defender now has the option to parry and riposte

The attacker now has the option to retreat from the riposte

Level 3:

Building off of level 2

The attacker now has the option to feint disengage from the defender's parry

The defender has the same options plus retreating from the lunge after the disengage

This works best with epee, but could work with foil and sabre with modification

You have to have a ref for this drill, even in epee

One fencer is just fencing a normal bout to 5

The other fencer has a specific target to hit, that the ref gave them

If they hit this target once, they automatically win

This could be modified for RoW weapons by using actions instead of targets

This is a bouting drill, done on a strip with electic scoring, fencers are fencing to ten

Once a fencer gets to 3,6, or 9 points, the other fencer gets to take an action away from the fencer that just scored

For newer fencers the action can be less specific or they could even take away a target area

For more experience fencers, the actions should be fairly complex

See the next section for valid and invalid action examples to take away

This section contains examples of valid and invalid actions to take away in the 3,6,9 Drill

Valid:

"You cannot parry 6 riposte" this is a valid action as the action is specific

"You cannot counter attack" this is valid, but could be more specific

"You cannot counter attack off my lunge" this is a better version of the previous example

"You cannot fleche in the line of 6" this is a good, specific action to take

"You cannot feint-disengage and riposte" very experienced fencers should use things like this

"You cannot hit my elbow" beginners can use things like this

"You cannot score on your end of the strip" this is technically valid, but should be avoided

Invalid:

"You cannot lunge" this is not valid, as it is not specific enough

"You cannot parry 6" this is also not specific enough

"You cannot advance" this is not specific enough
